Photo editing

Photo editing is the process of altering or enhancing a photograph
using various tools and techniques to improve its appearance, convey
a particular mood, or achieve a specific artistic effect. It can range from
simple adjustments to complex transformations. Here's a breakdown:

  1. Basic Adjustments

    • Brightness & Contrast: Changes how light or dark the image appears and the difference between the darkest and lightest areas.
    • Exposure: Adjusts the overall lightness of the photo.
    • Saturation & Vibrance: Controls the intensity of colors. Saturation affects all colors, while vibrance boosts less-saturated colors.
    • White Balance: Corrects color tones so whites appear white and the photo looks natural.

  2. Retouching

    • Blemish Removal: Removes imperfections like spots, wrinkles, or dust.
    • Skin Smoothing: Makes skin appear even and soft.
    • Red-Eye Correction: Fixes the red-eye effect caused by camera flash.

  3. Cropping & Resizing

    • Cropping: Removes unwanted parts of the photo and improves composition.
    • Resizing: Changes the photoâ€™s dimensions for printing or sharing online.

  4. Filters & Effects

    • Artistic Filters: Apply a stylized look like vintage, black-and-white, or HDR.
    • Blur & Sharpen: Emphasizes certain areas or softens parts of the image.
    • Vignettes & Shadows: Darken edges or enhance shadows to focus attention.

  5. Advanced Editing

    • Layering & Masking: Combines multiple images or selectively edits parts of an image.
    • Color Grading: Changes the overall tone or mood using color adjustments.
    • Object Removal or Replacement: Removes unwanted objects or adds new elements.
    • Compositing: Merges multiple images to create a single, cohesive picture.

  6. Output & Optimization

    • File Format Selection: JPEG, PNG, TIFF, etc., depending on use.
    • Resolution Adjustment: Ensures clarity for printing or digital use.
    • Sharpening for Display: Optimizes the image for online or print clarity.
